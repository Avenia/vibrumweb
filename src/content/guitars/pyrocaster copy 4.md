The Pyrocaster started with a question: what would a vintage-voiced single-cut feel like if every component was chosen for longevity and tone rather than spec-sheet appeal?

The body is swamp ash — a lighter cut with an open grain that rewards a proper nitrocellulose finish. The neck is hard maple with a comfortable C-profile, not too thick, not too thin. Indian rosewood for the fingerboard, chosen for a batch of blanks with tight, even grain.

Hardware is all nickel. The Gotoh SD90 tuners are locking without looking like it. The Wilkinson tremolo sits flush and returns to pitch reliably.

The pickups are Lollar Blackface in the neck and Blonde in the bridge — wound to vintage specs, clear and dynamic. They clean up well on the volume knob and break up naturally with the right amp.

Every detail was considered. Nothing was rushed.
